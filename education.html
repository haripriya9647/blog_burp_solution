The Right Kind of Education!!




Author Snehal Soni

Have you all wondered why there are crimes that are happening and increasing day after day. If a person commits a crime we blame him and forget that the crime he has committed today is not something that he has thought of instantly. There are many other factors that are involved when a crime is committed. However, the basic factor that is responsible for any crime happening around us remains to be “Education”.

Why Education? Because that’s where everything begins. Nobody is born criminal, but there are various factors that lead them to grow as a criminal. Our childhood education is the most vital factor which decides whether a person will become a good human being or a criminal. The psychological research of every criminal suggests that they had a troubled childhood due to which they either turn to be rebellious or become a silent sufferer. Childhood is the most crucial part of life, it is that part where your entire life starts folding in slowly and gradually.

It is said that the age group from 6 months to 8 years are very crucial for every child where parents, teachers, your peers, classmates, environment that they live in, contributes in developing the mindset of a child. Hence, education defines your personality. The right kind of education defines who you are and how you will grow as a person. Perhaps, that can change the entire world in the most productive way. The crisis that we face today is due to the fact that we have lacked somewhere in our education.
Integrated education is the need of an hour, and only that would save the world. Embrace the right kind of education in your life, and witness the change around you. Leaving you all, with a quote by a renowned leader of his time who promoted education to its fullest.

J. Krishnamurti, one of the greatest philosopher mentions in his book, Education and the Significance of life that, “Education should bring about the integration of the separate entities – for without integration, life becomes a series of conflicts &; sorrows.”

(I personally recommend this small handbook by J. Krishnamurti, who are passionate towards education)
